Starting Your Own Community Initiative

You don't need to be part of a large organization to make a difference. Many successful food waste initiatives start small and grow through community involvement and passion.

1

Identify Local Need

Research food insecurity and waste in your community. Talk to local restaurants, markets, and community centers to understand the landscape.

2

Build Your Team

Find like-minded individuals who share your passion. Start with friends, family, or neighbors who want to make a difference.

3

Partner with Local Businesses

Approach restaurants, grocery stores, and event venues about donating surplus food. Start small and build relationships.

4

Establish Distribution Network

Connect with local shelters, community centers, or families in need. Ensure safe, dignified food distribution.

5

Scale and Sustain

As your initiative grows, develop systems for sustainability, volunteer recruitment, and community education.
