Carbohydrates: Energy Powerhouses

Carbohydrates are the body's primary energy source, providing fuel for all bodily functions, especially brain activity and physical performance. Understanding different types of carbohydrates helps make informed food choices.

üìä Daily Requirements

Adults (19-30 years): 45-65% of total calories
Active individuals: 6-10g per kg body weight
Athletes: 7-12g per kg body weight

üåæ

Complex Carbohydrates

Sources: Whole grains, vegetables, legumes, nuts

Benefits: Sustained energy, fiber content, nutrient density

Examples:
  • Brown rice (1 cup): 45g carbs, 5g fiber
  • Sweet potato (medium): 27g carbs, 4g fiber
  • Oats (1 cup cooked): 27g carbs, 4g fiber
  • Quinoa (1 cup): 39g carbs, 5g fiber
üç¨

Simple Carbohydrates

Sources: Fruits, milk, processed sugars

Benefits: Quick energy, natural sweetness, vitamin content

Examples:
  • Banana (medium): 27g carbs, 3g fiber
  • Apple (medium): 25g carbs, 4g fiber
  • Milk (1 cup): 12g carbs, 0g fiber
  • Honey (1 tbsp): 17g carbs, 0g fiber
üç≠

Refined Carbohydrates

Sources: White flour, sugary drinks, processed foods

Concerns: Rapid blood sugar spikes, low nutrient density

Examples:
  • White bread (1 slice): 14g carbs, 1g fiber
  • Sugary soda (12 oz): 39g carbs, 0g fiber
  • White rice (1 cup): 53g carbs, 1g fiber
  • Pastries and cakes: High carbs, minimal fiber

Proteins: Building Blocks of Life

Proteins are essential for growth, repair, and maintenance of all body tissues. They play crucial roles in immune function, hormone production, and enzyme activity.

üìä Daily Requirements

Adults: 0.8g per kg body weight
Athletes: 2-2.0g per kg body weight
Older adults: 1.0-2g per kg body weight

ü•© Complete vs Incomplete Proteins

Complete Proteins

Contain all essential amino acids in adequate amounts

Sources:
  • Animal-based: Meat, fish, eggs, dairy
  • Plant-based: Quinoa, soybeans, chia seeds, hemp seeds

Incomplete Proteins

Missing one or more essential amino acids

Sources:
  • Most grains, legumes, nuts, and seeds
  • Can be combined to create complete proteins

Fats: Essential and Misunderstood

Despite their bad reputation, fats are essential for health. Understanding different types of fats and their functions helps make informed dietary choices.

üìä Daily Requirements

Adults: 20-35% of total calories
Essential fatty acids: Linoleic acid: 5-10% of calories
ALA (Omega-3): 0.6-2% of calories

üßà Types of Fats

ü•ë

Unsaturated Fats (Healthy)

Types: Monounsaturated and polyunsaturated fats

Benefits: Heart health, brain function, reduced inflammation

Sources:
  • Monounsaturated: Olive oil, avocados, nuts
  • Polyunsaturated: Fatty fish, flaxseeds, walnuts
  • Omega-3: Salmon, sardines, algae oil
  • Omega-6: Vegetable oils, nuts, seeds
üßà

Saturated Fats (Limit)

Sources: Animal products, coconut oil, palm oil

Recommendation: Less than 10% of total calories

Sources:
  • Red meat, full-fat dairy products
  • Coconut oil, palm kernel oil
  • Processed meats and fried foods
üö´

Trans Fats (Avoid)

Sources: Partially hydrogenated oils, processed foods

Recommendation: As close to 0% as possible

Sources:
  • Commercially fried foods
  • Packaged baked goods
  • Some margarines and shortenings
